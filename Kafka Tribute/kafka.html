Franz Kafka

"You are free and that is why you are lost."


Time line of Franz Kafka's life:
  • 1883 - Born in Prague, Austria-Hungary (now the Czech Republic).
  • 1889 - Attends the Deutsche Knabenschule German boys' elementary school.
  • 1893 - Attends secondary school at the Altstädter Deutsches Gymnasium, a rigorous classics-oriented school within the Kinský Palace.
  • 1901 - Admitted to the Deutsche Karl-Ferdinands Universität of Prague. He began studying chemistry, but after two weeks switched to law.
  • 1906 - Receives his Doctor of Law degree and begins his year of obligatory unpaid serive as a law clerk for the civil and criminal courts.
  • 1907 - Works at Assicurazioni Generali, an insurance company, for nearly a year. The work schedule, 8 AM to 6 PM, made it difficult for him to concentrate on writing.
  • 1911 - Becomes partners with Karl Hermann to start the first asbestos factory in Prague known as Prager Asbestwerke Hermann & Co.
  • 1912 - Begins writing Amerika.
  • 1914 - Begins writing The Trial.
  • 1915 - Receives a draft notice for World War I, but is excused due to his work at the factory was considered essential government service.
  • 1917 - Diagnosed with Tuberculosis after attempting to join the military.
  • 1918 - Put on pension for his illness, which there was no cure for at the time, and spends most of the rest of his life in sanitoriums.
  • 1922 - Begins writing The Castle.
  • 1924 - Kafka dies in Dr. Hoffmann's sanitorium in Kierling. Cause of death is thought to be starvation due to his illness making it extremely painful to eat.
  • 1925 - The Trial is published.
  • 1926 - The Castle is published.
  • 1927 - Amerika is published.

This timeline only lists three of Kafka's works. For a full bibliography or more information on Kafka's life visit: Wikipedia
